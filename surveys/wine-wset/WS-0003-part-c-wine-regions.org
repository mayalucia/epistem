A structured survey of the world's principal wine regions, organised for
WSET Levels 1--4 study. Each section covers: classification system, key
appellations, principal grape varieties, climate, soil, notable producers,
and style of wines. WSET level tags indicate depth of coverage expected:

- *L1* -- awareness level (region exists, flagship grape)
- *L2* -- intermediate (key sub-regions, principal varieties, basic style)
- *L3* -- advanced (terroir, classification detail, producer knowledge)
- *L4* -- diploma (micro-climate, soil geology, winemaking philosophy, market position)

Sources are cited inline as footnote-style references and collected at the end.

* France                                                             :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: france
:END:

** Classification System -- AOC/AOP

France's quality wine system rests on the /Appellation d'Origine Controlee/
(AOC), aligned since 2008 with the EU's /Appellation d'Origine Protegee/
(AOP). The hierarchy ascends through three tiers [fn:winefolly-aoc] [fn:cellartours-fr]:

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------+------------------------------------------+-------------------------|
| Vin de France | Table wine, no geographic restriction | Vin de France |
| IGP | Regional indication (prev. Vin de Pays) | Pays d'Oc |
| AOP/AOC | Controlled origin -- grape, yield, method | Pauillac, Chablis |

As of 2025 there are 363 AOCs and 75 wine IGPs in France [fn:winefolly-aoc].

** Bordeaux                                                        :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: bordeaux
:END:

*** Left Bank vs Right Bank

The Gironde estuary and its tributaries (Garonne, Dordogne) divide Bordeaux
into two fundamentally different terroirs [fn:decanter-bordeaux] [fn:winespectator-1855]:

| Attribute | Left Bank | Right Bank |
|-------------------+------------------------------+------------------------------|
| Dominant grape | Cabernet Sauvignon | Merlot |
| Key communes | Pauillac, Margaux, St-Julien | Saint-Emilion, Pomerol |
| Soil | Alluvial gravel over limestone | Clay, limestone |
| Wine style | Structured, tannic, long-lived | Softer, rounder, earlier drinking |
| Classification | 1855 Classification | Saint-Emilion (1955, revised) |

*** The 1855 Classification

Commissioned by Napoleon III for the Exposition Universelle de Paris.
Brokers ranked chateaux by reputation and trading price into five /crus/
(growths). All five First Growths are Left Bank [fn:winespectator-1855]:

1. Chateau Lafite Rothschild (Pauillac)
2. Chateau Latour (Pauillac)
3. Chateau Margaux (Margaux)
4. Chateau Haut-Brion (Pessac-Leognan/Graves)
5. Chateau Mouton Rothschild (Pauillac) -- promoted from 2nd Growth in 1973

The Left Bank also uses the /Cru Bourgeois/ system (Medoc). The Right Bank's
Saint-Emilion classification (1955, periodically revised) and Pomerol (no
official classification) operate independently.

*** Climate and Soil

Maritime climate moderated by the Atlantic and Gironde estuary. Left Bank
gravels capture and radiate heat, favouring late-ripening Cabernet Sauvignon.
Right Bank clays retain moisture, suiting earlier-ripening Merlot
[fn:decanter-bordeaux].

*** Notable Producers

Petrus, Le Pin (Pomerol); Cheval Blanc, Ausone (Saint-Emilion); Lafite,
Latour, Margaux, Haut-Brion, Mouton (Medoc/Graves); Lynch-Bages, Pichon-Baron.

** Burgundy (Bourgogne)                                           :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: burgundy
:END:

*** Terroir-Based Classification

Unlike Bordeaux (estate-based), Burgundy classifies by vineyard site
(/climat/). The hierarchy [fn:winefolly-burgundy] [fn:sommtv-burgundy]:

| Level | Count | Character |
|-------------------+-------+---------------------------------------------|
| Regional | 7 | Bourgogne Rouge/Blanc |
| Village | 44 | e.g. Gevrey-Chambertin, Meursault |
| Premier Cru | 640 | Named vineyard plots on village label |
| Grand Cru | 33 | Own appellation (e.g. Romanee-Conti, Corton) |

*** Cote d'Or

Two sub-regions: *Cote de Nuits* (24 of 25 red Grand Crus -- Pinot Noir) and
*Cote de Beaune* (white Grand Crus -- Chardonnay; exception: Corton).
Best vineyards occupy mid-slope positions with optimal drainage and sun
exposure. Soils are marly limestone [fn:winefolly-burgundy].

*** Chablis

Northernmost Burgundy, cooler climate similar to Champagne. *Kimmeridgian
limestone* -- chalky, fossil-rich -- gives trademark minerality. 100%
Chardonnay. Grand Cru Chablis (7 vineyards) and Premier Cru sites produce
age-worthy, steely whites [fn:winesearcher-chablis].

*** Grape Varieties

- *Pinot Noir*: ~60% of Cote d'Or production (red)
- *Chardonnay*: Chablis, Cote de Beaune, Maconnais (white)
- Minor: Gamay (Beaujolais), Aligote

*** Notable Producers

Domaine de la Romanee-Conti, Domaine Leroy, Domaine Leflaive, Coche-Dury,
Raveneau (Chablis), Comtes Lafon, Roulot.

** Champagne                                                      :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: champagne
:END:

*** Region and Climate

Northernmost major French wine region (~90 miles east of Paris). Cool
continental climate at the limit of vine viability. Chalky /Kimmeridgian/
and /Belemnite/ soils provide drainage, heat retention, and mineral character
[fn:champagne-wiki] [fn:winesociety-champagne].

*** Five Districts

| District | Dominant Grape | Character |
|--------------------+----------------+------------------------------|
| Montagne de Reims | Pinot Noir | Structure, power |
| Vallee de la Marne | Pinot Meunier | Fruity, approachable |
| Cote des Blancs | Chardonnay | Finesse, elegance |
| Cote de Sezanne | Chardonnay | Riper, broader style |
| Aube (Bar-sur-Aube) | Pinot Noir | Fuller, earthier |

*** Method and Style

/Methode traditionnelle/: primary fermentation, blending (/assemblage/),
secondary fermentation in bottle, ageing on lees (minimum 15 months NV,
36 months vintage), riddling, disgorgement, dosage.

Non-vintage (NV) blends assembled from multiple vineyards and vintages.
Vintage Champagne in exceptional years. Blanc de Blancs (100% Chardonnay),
Blanc de Noirs (Pinot Noir/Meunier), Rose (blending or saignee).

*** Growers vs Houses

~5,000 growers make their own wine; ~14,000 sell grapes to houses. Major
houses: Moet et Chandon, Veuve Clicquot, Krug, Bollinger, Pol Roger, Louis
Roederer, Taittinger, Gosset (oldest, founded 1584), Ruinart (1729).
Notable grower-producers (/recoltant-manipulant/): Egly-Ouriet, Pierre
Peters, Jacques Selosse [fn:champagne-wiki] [fn:winesociety-champagne].

** Rhone Valley                                                    :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: rhone
:END:

*** Northern Rhone                                                       :L3:

Continental climate, granite slopes. Syrah as sole red grape. Only ~5% of
Rhone production but the most prestigious appellations [fn:rhone-wiki]:

| Appellation | Style |
|------------------+-----------------------------------------------|
| Cote-Rotie | Elegant Syrah, up to 20% Viognier co-ferment |
| Hermitage | Powerful, tannic, long-lived Syrah |
| Cornas | Dense, dark, 100% Syrah |
| Saint-Joseph | Medium-bodied Syrah |
| Condrieu | Aromatic Viognier (white) |
| Chateau-Grillet | Single-estate Viognier |

Notable producers: E. Guigal, Jean-Louis Chave, Chapoutier, Paul Jaboulet Aine.

*** Southern Rhone                                                       :L2:

Mediterranean climate, rocky/sandy/alluvial soils. Blending culture --
Grenache, Syrah, Mourvedre (GSM) plus Cinsault and others [fn:rhone-wiki]:

| Appellation | Notes |
|------------------------+----------------------------------------------|
| Chateauneuf-du-Pape | Up to 13 permitted varieties; Grenache ~72% |
| Gigondas | Grenache-dominant, structured |
| Vacqueyras | Similar to Gigondas, value |
| Cotes du Rhone | Regional; vast volume, reliable quality |
| Tavel | Rose only (Grenache, Cinsault) |

Notable: Chateau Rayas, Beaucastel, Vieux Telegraphe, Clos des Papes.

** Loire Valley                                                    :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: loire
:END:

France's longest river; diverse climate from maritime (west) to continental
(east). Three principal grapes dominate by sub-region [fn:loire-wiki]:

| Sub-region | Key AOC | Grape | Style |
|------------------+-----------------------+-----------------------+---------------------------------|
| Pays Nantais | Muscadet | Melon de Bourgogne | Light, mineral, sur lie |
| Anjou-Saumur | Savennieres, Bonnezeaux | Chenin Blanc | Dry to lusciously sweet |
| Touraine | Vouvray, Chinon | Chenin Blanc / Cab Franc | Sparkling, still; elegant reds |
| Central Vineyards | Sancerre, Pouilly-Fume | Sauvignon Blanc | Crisp, flinty, herbaceous |

Notable producers: Domaine Huet (Vouvray), Didier Dagueneau (Pouilly-Fume),
Nicolas Joly (Savennieres), Charles Joguet (Chinon).

** Alsace                                                          :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: alsace
:END:

Sheltered by the Vosges mountains -- one of France's driest, sunniest
regions. ~90% white wine. Varietal labelling (unusual for France)
[fn:alsace-wiki] [fn:winesociety-alsace]:

*** Noble Varieties (permitted in Grand Cru)

- Riesling -- steely, mineral, age-worthy
- Gewurztraminer -- aromatic, lychee, rose, often off-dry
- Pinot Gris -- rich, honeyed
- Muscat -- dry, grapey, aromatic

*** Special Designations

- *Grand Cru*: 51 named vineyard sites; noble varieties only
- *Vendange Tardive* (VT): late-harvest, elevated sugar; semi-sweet to sweet
- *Selection de Grains Nobles* (SGN): botrytis-affected berries; dessert wine
- *Cremant d'Alsace*: traditional-method sparkling

Notable producers: Trimbach, Hugel, Zind-Humbrecht, Weinbach, Marcel Deiss.

** Languedoc-Roussillon                                            :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: languedoc
:END:

France's largest wine-producing region (>700,000 acres). Mediterranean
climate. Historically bulk production, now undergoing quality revolution.
Largest producer of IGP, organic, and rose wines in France [fn:languedoc-wiki].

Key appellations: Corbieres, Minervois, Faugeres, Saint-Chinian, Fitou,
Pic Saint-Loup, La Clape. Principal grapes: Grenache, Syrah, Mourvedre,
Carignan, Cinsault (red); Picpoul, Marsanne, Roussanne, Grenache Blanc (white).

Roussillon: Maury, Banyuls, Rivesaltes (/Vins Doux Naturels/ -- fortified sweet).

** Provence                                                        :L1:L2:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: provence
:END:

World's leading source of premium dry rose (~88% of production). Mediterranean
climate, mistral wind. Appellations: Cotes de Provence, Bandol (serious reds
from Mourvedre), Cassis, Palette. Grapes: Grenache, Cinsault, Mourvedre,
Syrah, Rolle (Vermentino).

* Italy                                                              :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: italy
:END:

** Classification System -- DOCG/DOC/IGT

Italy's quality pyramid mirrors but differs from France's
[fn:masterclass-italy] [fn:decanter-italy]:

| Level | Count | Description |
|------+-------+-------------------------------------------------|
| VdT | -- | Vino da Tavola (table wine, minimal regulation) |
| IGT | 118 | Indicazione Geografica Tipica (regional, flexible) |
| DOC | 329 | Denominazione di Origine Controllata |
| DOCG | 77 | ...Controllata e Garantita (highest; govt tasting panel) |

The IGT category (est. 1992) is significant: many prestigious wines
(Super Tuscans) chose IGT for creative freedom outside DOC grape restrictions.

** Piedmont                                                        :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: piedmont
:END:

Northwest Italy; 16 DOCGs (most of any region). Continental climate with
autumn fog in the Langhe hills [fn:decanter-barolo]:

*** Barolo DOCG

- Grape: 100% Nebbiolo
- Ageing: minimum 38 months (18 in oak); Riserva 62 months
- Soil: calcareous marl (Tortonian in La Morra/Barolo; Helvetian in
  Serralunga/Castiglione Falletto)
- Style: powerful, tannic, complex; tar, roses, dried cherry, truffle
- Notable: Giacomo Conterno, Bruno Giacosa, Bartolo Mascarello, G.D. Vajra,
  Vietti, Aldo Conterno

*** Barbaresco DOCG

- Grape: 100% Nebbiolo
- Ageing: minimum 26 months (9 in oak)
- Style: more perfumed, softer tannins, earlier approachability than Barolo
- Notable: Gaja, Produttori del Barbaresco, Bruno Giacosa, Roagna

*** Other Piedmont DOCG

Gavi (Cortese), Asti/Moscato d'Asti (Moscato Bianco), Barbera d'Asti,
Roero (Nebbiolo/Arneis).

** Tuscany                                                         :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: tuscany
:END:

*** Chianti Classico DOCG

- Grape: minimum 80% Sangiovese (up to 100%)
- Sub-zone between Florence and Siena; /Gran Selezione/ tier since 2014
- Style: cherry, herbs, firm acidity, moderate tannin
- Notable: Fontodi, Isole e Olena, Castello di Ama, Felsina

*** Brunello di Montalcino DOCG

- Grape: 100% Sangiovese Grosso (clone of Sangiovese)
- Ageing: 5 years minimum (2 in oak); Riserva 6 years
- Style: powerful, structured, long-lived; dried cherry, leather, earth
- Notable: Biondi-Santi, Soldera, Il Poggione, Casanova di Neri

*** Super Tuscans

Born in the 1970s--80s when producers used Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, or
non-traditional Sangiovese blends outside DOC rules -- classified as IGT
(then VdT). Pioneering wines: Sassicaia (Bolgheri), Tignanello, Ornellaia,
Solaia. Bolgheri DOC was later created partly to accommodate these wines.

** Veneto                                                          :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: veneto
:END:

*** Valpolicella and Amarone

- *Valpolicella DOC*: light, cherry-fruited red from Corvina, Corvinone,
  Rondinella
- *Amarone della Valpolicella DOCG*: dried-grape (/appassimento/) wine;
  rich, powerful, 15--16% ABV; dried fruit, chocolate, spice
- *Ripasso*: Valpolicella re-fermented on Amarone pomace; richer mid-weight
- Notable: Allegrini, Dal Forno Romano, Giuseppe Quintarelli, Masi

*** Prosecco

- *Prosecco DOC*: large zone (Veneto + Friuli Venezia Giulia); Glera grape;
  Charmat/tank method; fruity, floral, light
- *Conegliano Valdobbiadene DOCG*: superior hill-grown Prosecco; /Rive/
  single-vineyard and /Cartizze/ sub-zone

** Sicily and Alto Adige                                           :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: sicily-altoadige
:END:

*** Sicily

Mediterranean island; volcanic soils (Etna). Rising quality. Key grapes:
Nero d'Avola, Nerello Mascalese (Etna Rosso), Carricante (Etna Bianco),
Grillo, Catarratto. Etna DOC is the prestige appellation.

*** Alto Adige (Sudtirol)

Alpine, cool-climate region at Italy's northern border with Austria.
Exceptional whites: Pinot Grigio, Gewurztraminer, Sauvignon Blanc.
Reds: Schiava, Lagrein, Pinot Nero. German-speaking culture; dual labelling.

* Spain                                                              :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: spain
:END:

** Classification System -- DO/DOCa

Spain's /Denominacion de Origen/ (DO) system parallels France's AOC
[fn:winescholarguild-spain] [fn:winetraveler-spain]:

| Level | Description |
|---------------------+-----------------------------------------------------|
| Vino de Mesa | Table wine |
| Vino de la Tierra | Regional wine (~IGP) |
| DO | Denominacion de Origen (majority of quality wines) |
| DOCa / DOQ | Denominacion de Origen Calificada (highest) |
| Vino de Pago | Single-estate classification |

Only two DOCa/DOQ exist: *Rioja* (1991) and *Priorat* (2003).

** Rioja DOCa                                                      :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: rioja
:END:

Along the Ebro River, northeast Spain. Three sub-zones: Rioja Alta (higher,
cooler, clay-limestone), Rioja Alavesa (similar, Basque side), Rioja Oriental
(warmer, alluvial, Garnacha) [fn:rioja-wiki].

*** Grapes
Tempranillo (dominant), Garnacha, Graciano, Mazuelo (Carignan); whites:
Viura (Macabeo), Malvasia, Garnacha Blanca.

*** Ageing Classification

| Category | Total ageing | Oak minimum |
|---------------------+--------------+-------------|
| Joven | <1 year | None/minimal |
| Crianza | 2 years | 1 year oak |
| Reserva | 3 years | 1 year oak |
| Gran Reserva | 5 years | 2 years oak |

Notable: Lopez de Heredia, La Rioja Alta, CVNE, Muga, Roda, Artadi,
Marques de Murrieta.

** Ribera del Duero DO                                             :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: ribera
:END:

High plateau (~800m), extreme continental climate (hot days, cold nights).
Tinto Fino (Tempranillo) dominant. Powerful, concentrated, dark-fruited reds.
Notable: Vega Sicilia (Unico), Pingus, Pesquera, Emilio Moro.

** Priorat DOCa/DOQ                                                    :L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: priorat
:END:

Catalonia; steep terraced vineyards on /llicorella/ (slate/schist) soil.
Very low yields produce intensely concentrated wines. Garnacha and Carinena
(Carignan) dominant, often blended with Syrah and Cabernet Sauvignon.
Notable: Alvaro Palacios (L'Ermita), Clos Mogador, Clos Erasmus
[fn:priorat-classification].

** Jerez (Sherry)                                                 :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: sherry
:END:

Andalusia, southwest Spain. /Marco de Jerez/ triangle: Jerez de la Frontera,
El Puerto de Santa Maria, Sanlucar de Barrameda. Hot, dry climate; /albariza/
(chalk) soils.

*** Styles (via /Solera/ fractional blending system)

| Style | Character |
|-------------------+---------------------------------------------------|
| Fino | Pale, dry, yeasty, aged under /flor/ |
| Manzanilla | Fino from Sanlucar; lighter, saltier |
| Amontillado | Begins as Fino, then oxidative ageing |
| Oloroso | No flor; oxidative, rich, nutty, dry |
| Palo Cortado | Rare; finesse of Amontillado + body of Oloroso |
| Pedro Ximenez (PX) | Sweet, from sun-dried PX grapes |

Grapes: Palomino (primary), Pedro Ximenez, Moscatel.
Notable: Gonzalez Byass (Tio Pepe), Lustau, Valdespino, Equipo Navazos.

** Rias Baixas DO                                                  :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: rias-baixas
:END:

Galicia, northwest Spain. Cool, rainy, maritime. Albarino grape: crisp,
aromatic, stone-fruit, saline whites. Granite soils. Pergola (/parral/)
trellising. Notable: Pazo de Senorans, Do Ferreiro, Zarate.

** Cava                                                            :L1:L2:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: cava
:END:

Traditional-method sparkling wine, primarily from Penedes (Catalonia).
Grapes: Macabeo, Parellada, Xarel-lo. New /Cava de Paraje Calificado/ tier
for single-vineyard, long-aged wines. Notable: Gramona, Raventos i Blanc,
Recaredo.

** Classification System

| Level | Description |
|----------------------------+--------------------------------------|
| Vinho | Table wine |
| Vinho Regional (IGP) | Regional indication |
| DOC | Denominacao de Origem Controlada |

250+ indigenous grape varieties; strong tradition of field blends
[fn:winefolly-portugal].

** Douro and Port                                                  :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: douro-port
:END:

Steep terraced vineyards along the Douro River; schist soils; extreme
continental climate. Table wines (Douro DOC) increasingly prestigious
alongside fortified Port [fn:winefolly-portugal].

*** Port Styles

| Style | Character |
|-------------------+-----------------------------------------------------|
| Ruby | Young, fruity, blended |
| Reserve/LBV | Richer, 4--6 years in cask |
| Vintage/Vintage Port | Single vintage, bottle-aged |
| Tawny (10/20/30/40yr) | Oxidative, nutty, caramel, blended ages |
| Colheita | Single-vintage tawny, long cask ageing |
| Vintage (Vintage) | Flagship; declared ~3 times/decade; bottle-aged 20yr+ |

Key grapes: Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz (Tempranillo),
Tinta Barroca, Tinto Cao.

Notable: Taylor's, Fonseca, Graham's, Dow's, Niepoort, Quinta do Noval.

** Dao DOC                                                              :L3:

Granite soils, high altitude, continental climate ("Burgundy of the South").
Touriga Nacional dominant; elegant, balanced reds with ageing potential.
Notable: Alvaro Castro, Antonio Madeira.

** Alentejo DOC                                                    :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: alentejo
:END:

Hot, flat, southern plains -- Portugal's "California." Modern, fruit-forward
reds with generous oak. Aragonez (Tempranillo), Trincadeira, Alicante
Bouschet. Notable: Herdade do Esporao, Cortes de Cima, Mouchao.

** Vinho Verde DOC                                                 :L1:L2:

Northwest; cool, maritime, granite soils. Light, crisp, often slightly
spritzante whites. Alvarinho (Albarino) in Moncao e Melgaco sub-region
produces the most serious expressions. Also Loureiro, Trajadura.

** Madeira DOC                                                          :L3:

Atlantic island; volcanic soils. Fortified wines aged via /estufagem/ (heating)
or /canteiro/ (natural warmth). Four noble varieties define styles: Sercial
(dry), Verdelho (medium-dry), Bual (medium-sweet), Malmsey/Malvasia (sweet).
Virtually indestructible wines -- can age for centuries.
Notable: Blandy's, Henriques & Henriques, Barbeito.

* Germany                                                            :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: germany
:END:

** Classification Systems

Germany operates two parallel quality frameworks [fn:vdp-classification] [fn:wset-riesling]:

*** Official Pradikat System

Based on grape ripeness at harvest (must weight in degrees Oechsle):

| Pradikat | Oechsle | Character |
|---------------------------+---------+-----------------------------------------|
| Kabinett | 67--82 | Light, delicate, low alcohol |
| Spatlese | 76--90 | Late harvest, riper, more body |
| Auslese | 83--100 | Selected bunches, often botrytis-touched |
| Beerenauslese (BA) | 110--128 | Botrytis, sweet, concentrated |
| Trockenbeerenauslese (TBA) | 150--154 | Shrivelled botrytis berries, nectar-like |
| Eiswein | 110--128 | Frozen grapes, intense acidity + sweetness |

Wines may be dry (/trocken/), off-dry (/halbtrocken/feinherb/), or sweet.

*** VDP Classification (Private, ~200 Members)

Modelled on 19th-century Prussian vineyard tax maps; Burgundian hierarchy
[fn:vdp-classification]:

| VDP Level | Analogy |
|-------------------+---------------------|
| VDP.Gutswein | Regional |
| VDP.Ortswein | Village |
| VDP.Erste Lage | Premier Cru |
| VDP.Grosse Lage | Grand Cru |

/VDP.Grosse Lage/ dry wines labelled *Grosses Gewachs* (GG).

** Mosel                                                           :L2:L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: mosel
:END:

Steep slate slopes along the Mosel, Saar, and Ruwer rivers. Cool climate;
Riesling ~60% of plantings. Wines characterised by lightness (often 7--9%
ABV), laser acidity, and slate minerality. Sweet and dry styles.
Notable: Joh. Jos. Prum, Egon Muller, Markus Molitor, Willi Schaefer,
Fritz Haag, Clemens Busch.

** Rheingau                                                        :L2:L3:

South-facing slopes along the Rhine; warmer than Mosel. Riesling dominant.
Fuller body, riper fruit than Mosel. The /Charta/ association promoted dry
Riesling styles from the 1980s. Schloss Johannisberg, Robert Weil, Kunstler,
Leitz.

** Pfalz (Palatinate)                                              :L2:L3:

Germany's warmest major region; diverse soils (limestone, sandstone, basalt).
Riesling plus substantial Pinot Noir (/Spatburgunder/) production.
Notable: Muller-Catoir, Burklin-Wolf, Reichsrat von Buhl, Philipp Kuhn.

* Austria                                                            :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: austria
:END:

** Wachau                                                          :L3:L4:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: wachau
:END:

35 km stretch of the Danube; steep terraced vineyards on gneiss and
granite. Continental climate with Pannonian warmth from the east and
Atlantic coolness from the west [fn:vineawachau] [fn:winesearcher-austria]:

*** Vinea Wachau Classification (Independent of National Law)

| Category | ABV | Style |
|---------------+----------+----------------------------------------------|
| Steinfeder | <= 11.5% | Lightest; named after feathery steppe grass |
| Federspiel | 11.5--12.5% | Mid-weight; falconry reference |
| Smaragd | >= 12.5% | Richest; named after emerald lizard |

Primary grapes: Gruner Veltliner, Riesling.
Notable: F.X. Pichler, Emmerich Knoll, Prager, Hirtzberger, Domane Wachau.

** Kamptal                                                              :L3:

Valley of the river Kamp, north of Krems. Loess and volcanic soils.
Gruner Veltliner and Riesling. DAC system (Districtus Austriae Controllatus)
ensures origin-based labelling. Notable: Brundlmayer, Schloss Gobelsburg,
Jurtschitsch.

** Burgenland                                                      :L2:L3:

Hot Pannonian climate around Lake Neusiedl. Red wine focus: Blaufrankisch
(Lemberger), Zweigelt, St. Laurent. Also renowned for Beerenauslese and
Trockenbeerenauslese dessert wines, including the historic /Ruster Ausbruch/.
Notable: Heinrich, Moric (Blaufrankisch specialist), Kracher (sweet wines).

** United States                                                   :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: usa
:END:

*** Napa Valley, California                                        :L2:L3:

Warm Mediterranean climate; diverse soils (volcanic, alluvial, clay).
Cabernet Sauvignon is king (~50% of plantings). Sub-AVAs: Oakville,
Rutherford, Stags Leap District, Howell Mountain, Mt. Veeder.
Style: opulent, ripe, structured, often 14--15% ABV.
Notable: Opus One, Screaming Eagle, Harlan Estate, Stag's Leap Wine Cellars,
Ridge (Monte Bello), Robert Mondavi [fn:winefolly-newworld].

*** Sonoma County, California                                      :L2:L3:

Cooler and more diverse than Napa. Key sub-AVAs: Russian River Valley
(Pinot Noir, Chardonnay), Dry Creek Valley (Zinfandel), Alexander Valley
(Cabernet Sauvignon), Sonoma Coast (cool-climate Pinot Noir, Chardonnay).
Notable: Williams Selyem, Kistler, Ridge, Littorai.

*** Oregon -- Willamette Valley                                    :L2:L3:

Cool maritime climate (Burgundy parallel). 70% Pinot Noir. Sub-AVAs:
Dundee Hills, Eola-Amity Hills, Chehalem Mountains. Volcanic (Jory) and
sedimentary soils [fn:oregon-wiki].
Notable: Domaine Drouhin Oregon, Eyrie Vineyards, Bergstrom, Evening Land.

*** Washington State -- Columbia Valley                            :L2:L3:

Continental climate; 99% of WA wine. Rain shadow east of Cascades;
irrigated vineyards on basalt and loess. Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot,
Syrah, Riesling. Sub-AVAs: Yakima Valley, Walla Walla, Red Mountain,
Horse Heaven Hills.
Notable: Quilceda Creek, Leonetti, L'Ecole No 41, Charles Smith.

** Australia                                                       :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: australia
:END:

*** Barossa Valley, South Australia                                :L2:L3:

Warm continental; deep alluvial soils. Australia's most celebrated region
for Shiraz -- bold, powerful, ripe fruit, often from old vines (some 150+
years). Also Grenache, Mourvedre, Cabernet Sauvignon, Riesling (Eden Valley).
Notable: Penfolds (Grange), Henschke (Hill of Grace), Torbreck, John Duval.

*** McLaren Vale, South Australia                                       :L3:

Mediterranean maritime; sandy loam over limestone. Shiraz, Grenache,
Cabernet Sauvignon. Fuller, darker wines than Barossa with coastal influence.
Notable: d'Arenberg, Wirra Wirra, Clarendon Hills, Yangarra.

*** Margaret River, Western Australia                              :L2:L3:

Maritime (Indian Ocean influence); gravelly loam over granite/gneiss.
Only 3% of Australian production but ~20% of premium wine. Bordeaux-style
Cabernet/Merlot blends and Chardonnay excel.
Notable: Leeuwin Estate, Cullen, Vasse Felix, Moss Wood.

*** Hunter Valley, NSW                                                  :L3:

Subtropical; humid. Famous for age-worthy Semillon (picked early, low
alcohol, develops toast and honey with 10--20 years) and Shiraz.
Notable: Tyrrell's, Brokenwood, Mount Pleasant.

** New Zealand                                                     :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: nz
:END:

*** Marlborough                                                    :L1:L2:

Cool maritime; stony, alluvial soils. World benchmark for Sauvignon Blanc --
pungent, herbaceous, tropical, high acidity. Also Pinot Noir, Chardonnay.
Notable: Cloudy Bay, Dog Point, Greywacke, Craggy Range.

*** Central Otago                                                  :L2:L3:

World's southernmost commercial wine region. Continental, semi-arid;
schist soils. ~70% Pinot Noir -- intense colour, dark fruit, spice.
Notable: Felton Road, Rippon, Mt Difficulty, Burn Cottage
[fn:centralotago-wiki].

** Chile                                                           :L1:L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: chile
:END:

Long, thin strip between Pacific and Andes. Mediterranean climate;
phylloxera-free. Key regions [fn:winesofchile]:

| Region | Climate | Signature |
|--------------------+--------------------+-------------------------------------|
| Maipo Valley | Warm, continental | Cabernet Sauvignon (Alto Maipo) |
| Colchagua Valley | Warm | Carmenere, Cabernet, Syrah |
| Casablanca Valley | Cool, coastal | Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Pinot |
| Leyda Valley | Cool, coastal | Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Noir |
| Aconcagua | Warm to cool | Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah |

Carmenere is Chile's signature grape (originally from Bordeaux, thought
extinct until rediscovered in Chilean vineyards in 1994).
Notable: Concha y Toro (Don Melchor), Errazuriz, Montes, Almaviva, Clos
Apalta (Lapostolle).

** Argentina                                                       :L1:L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: argentina
:END:

*** Mendoza                                                        :L2:L3:

75% of Argentine wine. High altitude (600--1,500m); semi-arid desert
irrigated by Andean snowmelt. Alluvial soils. Extreme diurnal temperature
variation preserves acidity [fn:winefolly-argentina]:

- *Lujan de Cuyo*: traditional Malbec heartland
- *Uco Valley*: newer, higher (900--1,200m); intense colour, fresh acidity,
  mineral Malbec. Sub-regions: Tupungato, Tunuyan, San Carlos
- *Maipu*: warmer, reliable Malbec and Bonarda

Notable: Catena Zapata (Adrianna Vineyard), Achaval-Ferrer, Zuccardi,
Terrazas de los Andes.

*** Salta (Cafayate)                                                    :L3:

Extreme altitude (up to 3,000m) -- among world's highest vineyards. Torrontes
(aromatic white): floral, grapey, refreshing. Also Malbec at altitude.

** South Africa                                                    :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: south-africa
:END:

Wine Regions concentrated in Western Cape. Classification uses
/Wine of Origin/ (WO) system [fn:winefolly-sa]:

*** Stellenbosch                                                   :L2:L3:

Birthplace of Pinotage. Diverse soils (granite, sandstone, shale).
Mediterranean climate. Cabernet Sauvignon, Bordeaux blends, Chenin Blanc.
Notable: Kanonkop, Meerlust, Rustenberg, Thelema, Rust en Vrede.

*** Swartland                                                           :L3:

Revolution region -- old-vine, dry-farmed Chenin Blanc, Rhone-style reds
(Syrah, Grenache, Mourvedre). Minimal intervention philosophy.
Notable: Eben Sadie (Columella, Palladius), Mullineux, AA Badenhorst,
Porseleinberg.

*** Walker Bay                                                          :L3:

Cool maritime (Hemel-en-Aarde Valley). Cool-climate Pinot Noir and
Chardonnay. South Africa's answer to Burgundy.
Notable: Hamilton Russell, Bouchard Finlayson, Crystallum, Storm.

*** Pinotage

South Africa's unique cross of Pinot Noir x Cinsault (created 1925).
Ranges from smoky/earthy to plummy/chocolatey. Best examples from
Stellenbosch and Swartland.

:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: emerging
:END:

** Greece                                                          :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: greece
:END:

3,000+ years of winemaking; renaissance underway with modern techniques
applied to indigenous varieties [fn:winefolly-greece]:

| Region | Grape | Style |
|-------------------+------------------+-------------------------------------------|
| Santorini | Assyrtiko | Volcanic, high acidity, mineral, saline |
| Naoussa | Xinomavro | Structured, tannic ("black acid"); ages well |
| Nemea | Agiorgitiko | Medium-bodied, soft, dark-fruited |
| Mantinia | Moschofilero | Aromatic, pale pink, floral |

Santorini vines trained in /kouloura/ (basket) to protect from wind.
Notable: Domaine Sigalas, Gaia Wines, Kir-Yianni, Alpha Estate.

** Georgia                                                         :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: georgia
:END:

Oldest continuous wine culture (~8,000 years). UNESCO-protected /qvevri/
tradition (clay vessels buried in ground for fermentation and ageing)
[fn:wset-georgia]:

- *Kakheti*: largest region (~70% of production); qvevri heartland
- *Saperavi*: principal red grape -- deep-coloured, tannic, winter-hardy
- *Rkatsiteli*: principal white -- most ancient; versatile (dry to sweet)
- *Amber/Orange wine*: white grapes fermented on skins in qvevri for weeks
  to months; tannin structure, oxidative complexity, amber colour

Notable: Pheasant's Tears, Iago's Wine, Lapati Wines.

** Lebanon                                                         :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: lebanon
:END:

Ancient wine culture; modern industry from 19th century. *Bekaa Valley*:
high altitude (900--1,100m), Mediterranean continental climate, limestone
soils. French and Rhone varieties dominate: Cabernet Sauvignon, Cinsault,
Carignan, Syrah, Mourvedre [fn:sevenfifty-lebanon].

Chateau Musar (est. 1930): iconic; Cabernet Sauvignon, Carignan, Cinsault
blend aged 7+ years before release. Other: Chateau Kefraya, Chateau Ksara,
Domaine des Tourelles.

** England                                                         :L2:L3:
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: england
:END:

Cool-climate sparkling wine revolution. Chalk soils in *Sussex* and
*Hampshire* share geology with Champagne. Traditional method from
Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Meunier [fn:english-sparkling-wiki]:

- *Sussex PDO*: first English regional PDO (sparkling and still)
- Still wines from Bacchus (Sauvignon Blanc-like aromatic white)

Notable: Nyetimber (pioneered 1988), Ridgeview, Gusbourne, Wiston,
Chapel Down (Kent), Hambledon (Hampshire).

** Canada                                                               :L2:

*** Ontario (Niagara Peninsula)

Cool continental; lake-moderated. Riesling, Chardonnay, Pinot Noir,
Cabernet Franc. World's largest producer of *Icewine* (/Eiswein/).
VQA (Vintners Quality Alliance) classification.
Notable: Inniskillin, Tawse, Hidden Bench.

*** British Columbia (Okanagan Valley)

Semi-arid, lake-moderated; diverse microclimates. Pinot Noir, Merlot,
Syrah, Riesling. Emerging quality.

** China                                                                :L2:

Rapidly expanding; now an OIV member. Key regions: Ningxia (arid, continental;
Bordeaux varieties); Xinjiang; Yunnan (high altitude). Quality improving
significantly. Notable: Ao Yun (LVMH), Silver Heights, Grace Vineyard.
