* Scope and Method

This survey documents the *human journeys* to Diamer: who came, when,
why, what they wrote, and what happened to them. The organising axis is
chronological — each era contributes a layer to the palimpsest. The
same mountain infrastructure, read by different agents through different
models.

The output is raw material for a story — tentatively titled "The
Travellers to Diamer" — that takes the form of a guest register
spanning 10,000 years.

Method: web-accessible sources — primary texts (Chinese pilgrim accounts,
expedition memoirs, colonial ethnographies), academic publications,
mountaineering archives, news sources. Each part is self-contained with
its own consolidated sources section.

** What makes this survey different

WP-0012 and WP-0013 surveyed the *place* — geology, glaciology, ecology,
petroglyphs, spirit traditions. This WP surveys the *visitors* — a
diachronic cross-section of everyone who came to the mountain and left
a trace.

** Key findings

- *Dara Shikoh fort at Chilas* — resolved as a prestige misattribution.
  The fort was built by Ali Sher Khan Anchan (Maqpon dynasty, c. 1600),
  not the Mughal prince. (Part B)
- *Faxian's crossing* — direct quotation from Legge translation of
  the sāṅkupatha (peg-path) in the Indus gorge, 399 CE. (Part A)
- *The dead of Nanga Parbat* — all named from 1934 (10 dead) and 1937
  (16 dead), both European and Sherpa. Cumulative toll 1895–1953:
  29 dead. (Part C)
- *2013 massacre* — all 11 victims named. (Part D)
- *Shina love story found* — /Yurmas ga Malang/ by Khalifa Rehmat Jan
  Malang (1885–1971), comparable to Heer Ranjha. (Part E)
- *The structural silence* — the most documented mountain in the
  Karakoram has the least-documented local community. Seven compounding
  causes analysed. (Part E)
- *Diamer-Basha Dam* — 35,000+ petroglyphs threatened, only 14%
  being scanned. No comparable effort for oral traditions. (Part D)
